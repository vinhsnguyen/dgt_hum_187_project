Lower Class Life

Following the Vương Family

Next

A Boy and his Grandpa

Young, lower-income boy who goes to school spends time with his grandpa who is his main caretaker after school every day. His grandpa also needs his grandson's help to navigate life around the city

The grandson has to live his day to day without his parents because they are constantly working and are unable to take care of him at home so his grandpa needs to step in in order to make sure he is raised well and does his school work.

Next

A Playful Child

The grandfather is limited in his physical capabilities and has little money to bring his grandson to any sort of attractions, but he does what he can so that Duc is not just home helping with chores. He brings Duc on walks together, sharing humorous and loving moments of playfulness and care for one another.

Next

At the Market

The boy accompanies his Grandpa to the market sometimes, learning how to be street smart at a young age.

“Duc, learn from Grandpa. Half of the game is pure bluff, you need to look confident and rich when you want to haggle. No one does it better than your grandpa!”

Duc nods eagerly, and puffs his chest.

Next

Off to the Races

“Let's go, let's go to one, Grandpa!! Literally everyone goes!” The son is ecstatic to egg on his Grandpa, whose childlike curiosity has been piqued by the offerings of living in a city, miles from the rural rice fields he grew up among.

“Well, I've always been curious. Don't tell your parents I took you here…”

The son takes his grandpa to a horse race, a popular spectacle in the urban city.

Next

Duc Goes to School

Duc is elated to go to school! Everyone in his family highly values education, especially that of their first son. They splurge to buy him school supplied and new clothes, and Duc thinks that school will be so much fun.

Next

Duc's Education

Duc is morally righteous, but can't seem to focus on studies. Because of this, his classmates are fond of him, because he always stands up for the underdogs, and tries to keep class in order, just like how the teachers ask of them.

As Duc's grandpa puts it, his heart is there but his head isn't…

Next

Visiting the School

Grandpa visits the school principal with Duc because his parents are too busy to come. But the grandfather has never been at school before, all he knows is that education is something he never got to have, but that it's important — it's the only way up and out of poverty in this society.

“Duc is an enthusiastic student, he takes the teachers' words seriously…” the principal comments, looking at the student records. “But, he is not focused. How could you get a 40% on the last test? Is something at home distracting him?”

Duc looks blankly, “I focus on the teacher's words! I help discipline the other kids! I just, forgot that there was a test…”

Grandpa scolds Duc vehemently in front of the teacher, “How foolish of you! How embarrassing to be wasting Teach Truong's time! If you don't get better grades, see what will happen to you little rascal!” Deep down, Grandpa knows that the night before, Duc's father and mother got into an extreme fight about family finances. He knows that Duc is having a hard time…

Next

Grandpa's Story

While studying, Duc's grandpa reminisces about how he never got to go to school.

“When I was your age, I couldn't go to school. I started working from the moment I could remember and school wasn't an option,” he said. “Make sure to study hard and find work to take care of me and your parents.”

Before this, Duc rarely studied. But after hearing how his grandpa couldn't go to school, he began to study harder to make him and his family proud.

Next

Effort

Duc started school on the wrong foot, when he couldn't understand why people who he loved the most, like his grandpa, disciplined him the way he did for not performing well in grades.

Certainly it did not get easier, but after hearing his grandparent's vulnerable words of hope for him, he bit the bullet and committed himself to a life of studying.

In his world now, it is no longer the productivity of how much rice you can grow, but the ability to use logic and the power of knowledge to survive.

Next

His Journey Continues

Needs to get used to traveling by train since he doesn't have a car and needs to travel further as he gets older.

Fast forward 4 years, Duc finally got into the high school he wanted to go to but that school was too far for him and his grandpa to walk to everyday.

Now, he takes the train alone into the city on his first day of high school.

Duc thought nervously, “I hope the kids at school like me… I probably have nothing in common with them because I rarely go into the city.”
